Last updated: 1 January 2025

## 1. Purpose

Talent Fly International encourages the reporting of serious concerns about wrongdoing, including fraud, bribery, breach of law or policy, or other misconduct. This Whistleblowing Policy provides a safe and confidential channel for such reporting and protects those who report in good faith.

## 2. Scope

The policy applies to all staff, contractors, and (where appropriate) candidates and clients who wish to report a concern about Talent Fly International’s conduct or operations.

## 3. What to Report

Examples of concerns that may be reported under this policy include:

- Fraud or financial misconduct
- Bribery or corruption
- Breach of law or regulation
- Serious breach of our policies (e.g. data protection, equal opportunity, health and safety)
- Bullying, harassment, or discrimination
- Concealment of any of the above

## 4. How to Report

Reports may be made:

- **By email:** contact@talentflyinternational.com (subject: "Whistleblowing" or "Confidential Report")
- **In writing:** To the address on our website, marked confidential

You may report anonymously, but providing contact details can help us investigate and give you feedback. We will not disclose your identity without your consent unless required by law.

## 5. Process

We will acknowledge receipt (where contact details are provided), assess the concern, and investigate as appropriate. We will take corrective action where wrongdoing is found and will inform you of the outcome where possible without compromising confidentiality or legal obligations.

## 6. Protection from Retaliation

We do not tolerate retaliation (e.g. dismissal, demotion, harassment) against anyone who raises a concern in good faith. Anyone who retaliates may be subject to disciplinary action.

## 7. Malicious or False Reports

Reports made in good faith will not result in action against the reporter even if the concern is not upheld. However, making knowingly false or malicious reports may be treated as misconduct.
